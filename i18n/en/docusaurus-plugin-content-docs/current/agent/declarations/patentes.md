# Patente Contribution (CP) and Additional Centimes (CA)

**CGI reference:** Book 1, Title 2, Chapters 1-2 — Art. 91 to 129  
**Required permission:** `DECLARATION_PATENTE_CREATE`

---

## 1. Scope (Art. 91-93)

The patente is owed by any Djiboutian or foreign person carrying on in Djibouti a **trade, an industry or a profession** (Art. 91).

### Exemptions (Art. 100)

- State and public bodies
- Salaried civil servants, artists, teachers
- Midwives, breeders/farmers (own produce), fishermen
- Solo manual workers (without a shop)
- Cinema establishments

:::info Young entrepreneur exemption (LFI 2022)
Natural persons < 31 years, groupings of natural persons, voluntarily retired civil servants, dismissed employees (economic dismissal):
- Year 1: **100%** exemption
- Year 2: **50%** exemption
- Year 3: **25%** exemption
:::

---

## 2. Composition of the patente (Art. 103-105)

The contribution comprises **two elements**:

1. **Fixed duty**: owed by all patente holders, established per establishment and per activity
2. **Proportional duty**: owed only by classes 1 to 6 of the general schedule

---

## 3. The three types of patentes

### 3.1 Activity Patente

For traders, craftspeople and service providers. Calculated on the basis of:
- The **fixed duty** based on the activity class (determined by the ISIC code)
- The **proportional duty** based on the rental value of professional premises (classes 1–6 only)

:::info Automatic ISIC → Class mapping
SIGFID automatically performs the mapping between the taxpayer's ISIC code and their patente class.
:::

### 3.2 Importer Patente

For natural or legal persons importing goods. Calculated on the basis of annual imports.

### 3.3 Contractor Patente

For public and private works contractors. Calculated on the works turnover.

---

## 4. Annual character and pro rata temporis (Art. 94-99)

- Patente owed for the **full year** if activity exists on 1 January (Art. 94)
- Start during the year: patente due **from the first day of the start month** (Art. 95)
- Patentes are **personal** (Art. 97)

---

## 5. Additional Centimes (CA) (Art. 126-129)

Additional centimes are calculated as a percentage of the main patente. They are owed at the same time as the patente.

### Provisional instalments (Art. 282-3°)

Patente holders pay provisional instalments on the contribution for the current year.

---

## 6. Enter a patente return in SIGFID

1. Click **Returns → Patentes → New return**
2. Select the taxpayer
3. SIGFID automatically identifies the **type of patente** (Activity / Importer / Contractor) based on the ISIC code
4. Enter:
   - **Rental value** of professional premises (for the activity patente)
   - **Import volume** (importer patente)
   - **Works turnover** (contractor patente)
5. If activity starts during the year: SIGFID applies the **pro rata temporis**
6. SIGFID calculates fixed duty + proportional duty + additional centimes
7. Validate the return

---

## 7. Penalties and enforcement

| Infringement | Penalty | Article |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| Late payment | 0.5%/month + 5% | Art. 247 |
| Failure to file | 5%/month + 10% or 40% | Art. 246 |
| Operating without patente | Fine + NIF suspension | Art. 256 |

:::danger Tax clearance
Failure to pay the patente may lead to the **suspension of the NIF** and refusal of the **tax clearance certificate**, blocking all administrative operations of the taxpayer.
:::
